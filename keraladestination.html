Kerala

Kerala is situated on the southwestern tip of India. The state of Kerala is divided into hills and valleys, midland plains and coastal belts. The backwaters suitated along the coastal betls are one of the main tourist attraction of Kerala. Kerala is also popularly known as "Gods Own Country" because of its breathtaking natural beauty and greenary.

Place to Visit in Kerala

Munnar

Munnar is a breathtaking hill station in Kerala, India, known for its lush tea plantations, misty hills, and serene landscapes. It sits at an altitude of 1,600 meters above sea level and was once a summer retreat for the British government. The name "Munnar" means "three rivers" in Malayalam, as it is located at the confluence of the Muthirapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala river.

Wayanad

Wayanad is a stunning hill station in Kerala, known for its lush greenery, waterfalls, caves, and wildlife. It sits at an elevation ranging from 700 to 2,100 meters in the Western Ghats, offering a peaceful retreat surrounded by nature. The ideal time to explore Wayanad is October to May, when the weather is pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Thekkady

Thekkady is a mesmerizing hill station in Kerala, best known for its dense forests, spice plantations, and the famous Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary. It is located in the Idukki district at an altitude of 700-900 meters above sea level. The ideal time to explore Thekkady is September to May, when the weather is pleasant and perfect for wildlife safaris and sightseeing.
