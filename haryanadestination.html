Haryana

Haryana is a state in northern India, known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and strong agricultural base. It was carved out of Punjab in 1966 and has since become a hub for industry and technology, especially in cities like Gurugram and Faridabad. Haryana is also famous for its role in ancient Indian history, particularly as the land of Kurukshetra, where the epic battle of the Mahabharata took place.

Place to Visit in Haryana

Morni Hills

Morni Hills is the only hill station in Haryana, located near Panchkula, close to Chandigarh. It sits at an altitude of 1,220 meters (4,000 feet) and offers scenic views, trekking trails, and serene lakes. The hills are part of the Shivalik range and are known for their rich biodiversity, including birds like Crested Kingfisher, Himalayan Bulbul, and Kalij Pheasant.

Kalesar Forest Hills

Kalesar Hills is a scenic natural retreat located in the Yamunanagar district of Haryana, near the foothills of the Shivalik range. It is home to the Kalesar National Park, a protected area known for its dense forests, rich biodiversity, and wildlife safaris.

Panchkula Hills

Panchkula Hills is a scenic region in Haryana, located near Chandigarh, offering breathtaking landscapes and outdoor adventures. The most famous hill station in this area is Morni Hills, which is the only recognized hill station in Haryana.
